WORKSHOP ON STUDYING & TEACHING GOD’S WORD

PART ONE-“STUDYING GOD’S WORD”
Before anyone can adequately teach God’s Word, he or she must learn how to interpret God’s Word. This is a process of discovery wherein students inductively looks at the essential principles that God gave to the biblical author in ancient days, and now determine what principles are intended for the audience of today as transferable teachings from God. This workshop will introduce students to the most effective methods for understanding the main idea and principles of God’s Word to prepare to teach the Bible.

PART TWO-“TEACHING GOD’S WORD”
The art of studying God’s Word is the discovery of the main idea and the transferable principles of the text. In order for the text to be taught, the main idea and biblical principles must be synthesized into a lesson plan. This will aid students in learning what God was teaching during the ancient times, but also is relevant for learning and living godly lives today. This workshop will introduce students to the most effective methods for teaching God’s Word.

Understanding Self(Workshop at 3:30-5:00p.m. on Friday, 8/7/15)
Being understood is a lifelong yearning for every human being. Yet for many reasons, we may not understand and communicate who we really are. As dynamic, God designed human beings, we are shaped by our environment, family of origin, relationships and life experiences. Using examples of Matthew and others (Matthew 9:9; 1:117), find out who you are in the eyes of God. Understand yourself better by identifying past life events that led you to who you (and others) think you are today. Good News: You don’t have to be stuck there!

Dealing with Feelings (Workshop #1 at 10:15-11:15 a.m. Saturday, 8/8/15)
Each family must face all kinds of pressure. For families with members immigrated to the United States from overseas, there are additional cultural, language, and other factors that affect their family dynamic and interactions. How could individuals in a multiethnic, multicultural, and multigenerational family help themselves and one another to understand emotions and deal with feelings so that they could meet the needs of each other and live in intimate loving connection by God’s design? What can we learn from King David, the man after God's own heart, when he was depressed (Psalm 13)?

Help! Nobody is Listening! (Workshop #2 and #3 at 1:00-3:00 p.m. Saturday, 8/8/15) Are you frustrated that conversations with your spouse, parents and children seem to be going nowhere? Examine interactive styles and patterns that make or break communications. Explore key elements of twoway sharing according to James 1:19 in order to (re) build and deepen relationships while promoting personal awareness and growth. Learn how to listen so that others will talk, and how to talk so that they will listen. Mandarinspeaking parents are invited to dialog and learn with their youth.

Q&A Session in Mandarin for Parents (3:30-5: 00 p.m. on Saturday, 8/8/15)
Questions and Answers for Mandarin Speaking parents to process and apply the learning. Further explore parental goals and expectation, cultural impacts, value system and other paradigm shifts that will help parents to get along with, enjoy, and influence their ABC children for the long haul.

Addressing Today’s Challenges Towards the Bible
The Bible has undergone increasing criticism and attack in the recent years. The university traditionally has been the fertile ground for open attacks on the Bible, where a good percentage of those who grow up in the church begin to question, even toss aside altogether, their faith. Today, serious challenge begins as early as high school. While unavoidable, some challenges and objections are, however, “self- inflicted”—by how Christians mishandle the Bible. Many believers today continue to ignore figurative and symbolic language of the Bible, read their own theology into the Bible, and make the Bible say what it never intends to say. Christians who build their faith on a faulty view of the Bible put their faith in jeopardy and give the world a reason to discredit Christianity. What are the ways you might be mishandling the Bible? What are some of the practical ways you can begin to read the Bible responsibly so as to remove unnecessary conflicts with today’s world? These are the two main topics that this workshop will address. This workshop will help you develop a proper view of the Bible and fend off potential challenges to your faith.
